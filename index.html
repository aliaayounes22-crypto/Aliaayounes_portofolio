The Challenge

The client, a specialized home goods e-commerce brand, was spending EGP 18,000/month on Meta Ads with a ROAS of only 1.3× — barely covering ad spend. Core problems identified in the audit:

• No Pixel architecture — standard purchase events only, no micro-conversion tracking.
• Broad cold audiences averaging 8–12M people with no interest layering.
• Single-stage funnel — all budget pushed to conversion campaigns with no warm-up phase.
• Zero creative testing — one static image ad running for 4+ weeks.

The Engineering Approach

Phase 1 — Competitor Intelligence: Used Meta Ads Library to audit 12 direct competitors, mapping their creative formats, offer framing, and estimated audience segments. Identified a content gap in video-first product demonstrations.

Phase 2 — Technical Infrastructure: Rebuilt the Meta Pixel with 8 custom events (ViewContent → AddToCart → InitiateCheckout → Purchase). Implemented server-side API events to recover iOS 14.5+ signal loss and improve attribution accuracy by ~30%.

Phase 3 — Audience Engineering: Built a 3-tier audience stack — cold (LAL 2% from top purchasers), warm (video viewers 75%+ & website visitors 30d), hot (cart abandoners 7d). Each tier received distinct creative and bid strategy.

Execution & Tools

Campaign architecture was built and monitored entirely through Meta Ads Manager with a structured naming convention for scalable reporting. Google Analytics 4 was configured with custom e-commerce events to cross-validate Meta attribution and identify post-click drop-off points in the checkout funnel.

Ad creatives were produced in Canva — including 6 static variants and 4 video concepts — tested using ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization) before consolidating budget into CBO on the winning set. Copy was written using a Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS) framework validated against competitor messaging.

Results & Conclusion

By day 30, the restructured campaigns achieved a ROAS of 3.6× — a 177% improvement. By day 60, after iterative audience pruning and creative refresh cycles, ROAS stabilized at 4.2× with CAC dropping from EGP 140 to EGP 82 per customer.

The CTR on video creatives outperformed static ads by 3.4×, validating the competitor gap identified in the initial audit. Conversion rate climbed from 0.8% to 3.1% driven by the segmented retargeting architecture.

The engineering mindset was the differentiator — treating the campaign as a system to be debugged, not a creative to be guessed. Every variable was isolated, measured, and optimized in sequence rather than in parallel, enabling clean causal attribution of each performance gain.
